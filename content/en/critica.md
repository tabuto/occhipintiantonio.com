## «One of the finest watercolourists in Italy»

_1982 · Autograph letter · Pietro Annigoni_

![Autograph letter by Pietro Annigoni to Antonio Occhipinti, 1982](https://res.cloudinary.com/ds8hmzyom/image/upload/v1776512977/pyartist/web/personalit/lettera-autografa-p-annigoni-1982-20.jpg)

```
XVI · VII · LXXXII
PIETRO ANNIGONI
BORGO ALBIZI, 8 — TELEF. 212.438
50122 FIRENZE
```

> Dear Occhipinti,
>
> I cherish a fond memory of our meeting — a memory that is, moreover, made tangible by the fine portrait you made of me and so generously wished to give me as a gift, together with the beautiful watercolour of Gela.
>
> Looking at these two works of yours at my leisure, I notice that you are able to combine atmospherically vibrant, light-filled transparencies with a rare solidity, and — especially in the portrait — a corporeality that is so difficult to achieve in this technique. I feel I can say with complete confidence that you are one of the finest watercolourists in Italy.
>
> I thank you again and wish you much good work!

_Yours sincerely, **Pietro Annigoni**_

## Occhipinti as seen by Maestro Aldo Raimondi

_Milan_

> I would first like to express my satisfaction at having seen the works of Antonio Occhipinti, whom I have found to be proud of being a watercolourist — and rightly so, for watercolour is the most precious pictorial expression one can imagine. In the world it is difficult to find true watercolourists, since watercolour is a very demanding technique, more so than any other, more so than any other pictorial expression: it is not for everyone; one must be gifted by nature, just as Occhipinti is, and he represents this category very well.
>
> Fundamental is his ability to conceive his works in his mind and to know their outcome even before executing them.
>
> Observing his works, it is clear that he already possesses the governing principle of the art of watercolour — that is: «Stop in time» — not allowing himself to be carried away by reworkings or complications that would compromise the freshness which is the very essence of watercolour.

_**Aldo Raimondi**, Milan_

## Occhipinti as seen by Aldo Riso

_Mitreo, August 1991_


> Watercolour is a difficult technique, but when handled with mastery its results are stunning — results that no other method can achieve.
>
> Yet it is not only practice, not only the painter's great skill, nor technique alone, nor the transparency of colour: what is also needed is emotion, the feeling of sensing the colour, of perceiving the light touch and the translucent clarity of the brushstroke.
>
> A caress on paper, a gesture of love — like a letter from a lover to his beloved — that is watercolour; that is the result of a technique wed to artistic longing.
>
> And Antonio Occhipinti is a painter: he certainly has technique, he certainly has measure and a sense of proportion, he has an instinct for colour and the swift line of drawing — but above all he has a wonderfully light hand guided by a soul steeped in poetry, and that is what makes his work true art.
>
> Antonio possesses all of this; he lives within the reality of his figurative world, yet he conveys it as memory rather than visual reality, as poetry rather than portrait.
>
> A true artist who earns my full admiration as a fellow watercolourist, and above all my deepest respect — for the man and for the painter.

_**Aldo Riso**, Mitreo, August 1991_
