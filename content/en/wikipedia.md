**Antonio Occhipinti** (Gela, 1937 – Gela, July 2024) was an Italian painter, watercolourist, sculptor and portrait artist. Regarded as one of the most refined Italian watercolourists of the twentieth century, he devoted over sixty years to watercolour painting, taking part in hundreds of exhibitions in Italy and abroad and receiving prestigious national and international awards. The painter Pietro Annigoni described him as *"one of the best watercolourists in Italy"*.

---

## Biography

Antonio Occhipinti was born in Gela, Sicily, in 1937. He lived and worked in his home town throughout his life, becoming one of its most representative artists. His first documented exhibition dates to 1956, when he held a solo show at the Circolo S. Agostino in Gela.

Over the course of his long career he collaborated actively with the Municipality of Gela and other cultural bodies, organising group exhibitions, open-air painting events and artistic gatherings that brought some of Italy's and the world's most celebrated painters to Gela.

He died in July 2024 at the age of 87, leaving a vast artistic and cultural legacy.

---

## Style and technique

Occhipinti stood out for his mastery of watercolour, one of the most demanding and unforgiving techniques in painting. His style — refined and elegant — was characterised by a skilled handling of glazes and transparencies capable of capturing Mediterranean light and softening it into tonal nuances of a highly evocative expressive quality.

His stylistic research united the chromatic sensibility of southern Italy with a classically inspired compositional rigour, producing works of great formal balance. He also worked in sculpture and portraiture, a genre in which he achieved particular distinction, being commissioned to paint portraits of popes subsequently displayed in the Vatican Galleries.

---

## Artistic career

### Early years (1956–1969)

Occhipinti's career began in 1956 with his first solo exhibition in Gela. Throughout the 1960s he participated in various open-air painting events and group shows across Sicily, already distinguishing himself for the technical quality of his watercolours.

### National recognition (1970–1984)

During the 1970s the artist broadened his national presence, exhibiting in several Italian cities including Caltanissetta, Palermo, Florence, Rome, Vicenza, Viareggio and Milan. In **1982** he received the Gran Premio del Tirreno in Viareggio; in **1983** he won the **Premio Nazionale G.B. Tiepolo** in Venice and the Premio "Galleria Centro Storico" in Florence.

In 1985 he participated for the first time in the *"100 Watercolourists in Rome"* review hosted by the Banca d'Italia, an event he returned to several times in subsequent years.

### International awards (1985–1999)

1986 was a landmark year: Occhipinti placed **first** at both the **Premio Internazionale Fabriano** (Fabriano, AN) and the Premio Internazionale "La Sciara di Stromboli" (Aeolian Islands). During the same period he exhibited in Paris (1989, Galerie Jacqueline Nioncel) and in Rome, establishing himself as a leading figure in Italian watercolour painting.

In **1994** he received the **Silver Medal from the President of the Italian Republic Oscar Luigi Scalfaro** for his artistic career, awarded in Modica (RG). The following year he exhibited at the **Museo del Acquerello in Mexico City**, the first of a series of international appearances that took him to Belgium, Ireland, Hungary, Spain and the United States.

In **1998**, during an exhibition in Vatican City, he personally presented his portrait of **His Holiness Pope John Paul II**.

### International activity and mature years (2000–2009)

In the early 2000s Occhipinti took part in pan-European reviews, including *"Colori sull'Acqua"* (2003, Civico Museo Parisi Valle, Maccagno) with artists from Italy, Catalonia, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway and Sweden, and *"Thirty Years of Watercolour"* (2004, Museo Civico d'Arte Moderna e Contemporanea, Arezzo) with artists from fifteen nations.

In 2001 he held a solo exhibition curated by the painter **Piero Guccione** at the Galleria L'Androne in Scicli, entitled *"Espressioni Poetiche nell'Acquerello"* — a significant critical recognition of his work.

## Works in public collections

- **Vatican Galleries** — portraits of popes executed in his later years of activity.
- **Gela Municipal Art Gallery** — 22 watercolours on the history of Gela, painted between 1990 and 2015. The gallery has been dedicated to the artist following a proposal by the Centro di Cultura Zuppardo.
- **Churches of Gela** — numerous works of sacred character held in local churches.

---

## Associations

Occhipinti was an **Honorary Member of the Associazione Italiana Acquerellisti (A.I.A.)** for over forty years, until his death. The A.I.A. commemorated him with an official announcement on its website following his passing.
